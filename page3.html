CHARACTERS IN NOLI ME TANGERE


Juan Crisostomo y Magsalin (Ibarra)
  • Don Rafael Ibarra, a prosperous landowner in the Philippines, is the father of Crisostomo Ibarra.
  • He was raised in the Philippines where he was born and nurtured, leading a privileged and protected existence as a member of the Filipino elite. Ibarra's father sends him to Europe as a young man to pursue his studies.
Maria Clara
  • Mara Clara is portrayed in the novels as a devoted Roman Catholic who attained the pinnacle of virtue, becoming "demure and self-effacing," humorless, and prone to fainting.
  • She's the real daughter of Padre Damaso because Padre Damaso raped Doña Pía Alba.
Father Damaso
  • According to reports, Padre Dámaso is an extrovert who is haughty, merciless, and judgmental.
  • He lacks self-control when speaking and doesn't give a damn if the person he is addressing to feels ashamed or guilty.
  • He constantly criticizes or berates those around him, especially Ibarra.
 Capitan Tiago
  • A well-liked and respected socialite from the rich class of the Philippines.
  • Captain Tiago is one of the wealthiest landowners in Manila and San Diego and is close with prominent clergymen like Father Salv and Father Dámaso.
 Padre Salvi
  • A Spanish friar named Padre Salvi arrives in the Philippines to work for the Catholic Church.
  • The events of "Noli Me Tangere" take place in the imaginary town of San Diego, where he is assigned. The community recognizes Padre Salvi as a leading figure very fast.
 Pilosopo Tasio
  • Pilosopo Tasio was a wise and knowledgeable person. Tasio, who has always shown a passion for philosophy and education, allowed himself to fall into poverty so he could afford to buy so many books.
  • Tasio dedicated his life to learning.
